I am campaigning to be Norfolk's next Commonwealth's Attorney and the first female Commonwealth's Attorney in the City of Norfolk. On August 18, 1920, Tennessee became the last state needed to ratify the 19th Amendment to the Constitution guaranteeing the right to vote to women. We have come far in 100 years but have much further to go, which is why I chose this historic day to announce my candidacy.

I am very grateful to the community leaders, elected officials, friends, colleagues, and peers who have encouraged me while making this important decision. I would especially like to thank my long-time friend and criminal justice reform champion, Del. Joe Linsdey, for his support, mentorship, and encouragement in making this decision and for his confidence in my ability to best serve Norfolk’s citizens as their Commonwealth’s Attorney.

For information on how to join me on this campaign journey to the June 2021 democratic primary please view the rest of the information on this website or follow me on Facebook, Twitter, or Instagram. With your support, we can create safer communities, ensure equal justice for every citizen, and work for tirelessly for victims of crime in the City of Norfolk."
